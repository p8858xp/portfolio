LA is home. No matter where I go or where I am, I am proud to say that LA is my motherland. LA is the place where I learned to be myself and be the person I am today.

Golf

I was introduced to golf in LA and my life eventually came to revolve around it. I learned about the mentality to never give up.

Because of all the competitions that I competed in for golf, I got to travel to many places with my dad. One of my favorite memories I have is when I went to San Diego with him because it didn't even feel like I was there for a tournament. Instead, it was as though we ran away from home for a short vacation. Golf was, is, and always will be a huge part of my life. I love the experiences golf provided and I will never forget the memories we shared together.

LA to NY

After 18 years of living only in LA, I decided to attend NYU and spend the next 4 years of my life in NYC. I wanted to experience what it would be like to live in another state without my parents and I don't regret my decision at all. I came to appreciate my parents way more and be forever grateful for all things they have done for me back home. It's not easy to cook for yourself I unfortunately came to realize... NY has become my second home. I love its people, its community, and its rush.

RCF

New York City is the place that got me plugged into RCF. RCF or Remnant Christian Fellowship is a community I was lucky to come across and meet many brothers and sisters in Christ. I love being a part of this body and being able to serve alongside those who love God the same way that I do. Before I found RCF, I didn't really know who to hang out with and what I should do, but after being in RCF for a year now, the only word that I comes to mind is blessed. I can't wait for future plans God has planned for me next.
